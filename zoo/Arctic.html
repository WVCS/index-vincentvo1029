    Animals of the South Pole

    Welcome to the South Pole! The gravity-defying chunk of land that exists south of probably wherever you stand! Despite literally being an icy desert, there's a wide variety of life living in the South Pole. Come check it out!

    Contrary to popular belief, penguins and polar bears DO NOT coexist in the same habitat! Penguins live in the Southern Hemisphere while polar bears occupy the North. This zoo features animals exclusively in the South Pole so unfortunately, no polar bears! This is not bias on part of the coder, absolutely not.



































































    Additionally, puffins can be seen here! Puffins are cute little birds smaller than penguins but similar in characteristics. There's a major difference though; thanks to their small size and light bones, puffins can fly! They're adorable and pudgy, just look at them! They're so fat they need a running start before they can fly. That or they launch themselves off cliffs.

    Last but not least on this list of totally not biased arctic animals are the orcas. The KILLER WHALES. Oracas should not be kept in captivity (screw you, Sea World), so instead, we invite you to go on a whale watch to observe these amazing animals. If you have the opportunity to do so, do it and tell me how it went (ignore this sentence)! Also fun fact: Did you know that killer whales are more closely related to dolphins than they are actual whales?
